4) Mechanism clarification (causal claim to verify)
- Important clarification:
  - We do NOT claim "value learning directly causes endpoint diversity."
  - The more defensible chain is:
    1) value/Q learning improves action quality toward goal progress
    2) entropy/stochastic sampling occurs around high-value actions
    3) closed-loop correction keeps trajectories feasible
    4) this can produce broader endpoint spread with equal-or-better success
- Therefore, the conjecture is:
  - SAC's advantage comes from control-aware branching (quality-weighted stochasticity),
    not from larger raw action noise magnitude.

7) GPT Pro ablation grid review (2026-02-22, deferred for later action)
Source: GPT Pro analysis of 2026-02-21b experiment bundle
Status: DEFERRED — revisit after current online self-improvement experiments complete

Key issues found in prior ablation grid experiments:
a) Threshold confusion: grid_v1 "63.9% success" used ~0.5 threshold (coarse progress);
   grid_v2 uses 0.2 (strict). The +11pp gain was mostly about "getting close," not truly reaching the goal.
   Under strict 0.2 threshold, best Diffuser only improves +2.8pp over baseline.
b) RNG contamination: SAC baseline in analyze_collector_stochasticity.py shares RNG stream
   with Diffuser. SAC results vary across Diffuser conditions (0.5556 → 0.6944 in grid_v2),
   invalidating "gap closed by X%" conclusions.
c) Too few rollouts: 36 per condition (6 queries × 6 rollouts). A +5.6pp improvement
   is just +2 extra successes — easily noise.
d) EMA + adaptive replan negative interaction: At alpha=1.0, each helps alone (+2.8pp)
   but together they hurt (-5.6pp). Adaptive trigger fires too aggressively with
   EMA-smoothed progress signals.
e) Action scaling saturation: alpha=1.4 causes 53% clip fraction.

Proposed fix order (by GPT Pro):
  E0: Fix RNG isolation (must-do before any SAC comparison is valid)
  E1: Report success at multiple thresholds (0.5/0.2/0.1) simultaneously
  E2: Add control diagnostics (num_replans, adaptive_triggers, wall_hits, action_l2)
  E3: Replan cadence sweep (hypothesized as biggest real factor)
  E4: Near-goal tighter control heuristic (replan_every=1 when dist<0.5)
  E5: Re-run small ablation with bigger eval budget (~200 rollouts)
  E6: Only then: multi-seed swap matrix with best collector config
